Methodology

I

Data Pipeline

The AG News corpus provides 120,000 training and 7,600 test articles across four categories. Title and description fields are concatenated, lowercased, stripped of punctuation and digits, and filtered against a 130-term English stopword list. Tokens under three characters are discarded.

II

TF-IDF Vectorisation

Text is converted to a sparse numerical matrix using Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency weighting. The vectoriser retains 30,000 features, applies sublinear TF dampening (log(1+tf)), and extracts both unigrams and bigrams, capturing phrase-level signals such as "interest rate" or "world cup".

III

Naive Bayes

Multinomial Naive Bayes applies Bayes' theorem with a conditional independence assumption between features. Despite this simplification, NB is highly effective on high-dimensional sparse text data. Laplace smoothing (α=0.1) prevents zero-probability issues for unseen tokens.

IV

Logistic Regression

A linear classifier trained by maximising the log-likelihood of the multinomial distribution. L2 regularisation (C=5.0) prevents overfitting. LR produces well-calibrated probability scores, making it useful when confidence estimation matters. Solved via L-BFGS.

V

Linear SVC

Support Vector Classification with a linear kernel finds the maximum-margin hyperplane between classes. Unlike probabilistic models, SVC directly optimises classification margin. Platt scaling via cross-validated calibration wraps the classifier to produce probability estimates.

VI

SGD Classifier

Stochastic Gradient Descent with log-loss behaves identically to logistic regression but updates weights on individual samples. This makes it extremely fast and memory-efficient on large corpora. Calibrated with cross-validation for reliable probability output.
